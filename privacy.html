Data &
Privacy.

Therapy requires complete trust. Part of that trust is knowing exactly how your personal information is handled, stored, and protected.

1. Information Collection

When you enquire about therapy, I collect basic contact information (your name, email address, and phone number) so that we can communicate. During our work together, I keep brief, factual notes of our sessions. These notes serve as an aide-mémoire for my clinical practice and do not contain highly sensitive identifiable details.

2. Storage and Security

Your privacy is my priority. All digital information is stored securely on encrypted, password-protected devices. Any physical notes are kept in a locked filing cabinet, separate from your identifiable contact details. In line with UKCP and insurance guidelines, client records are retained securely for up to 7 years after our work concludes, after which they are permanently destroyed.

3. Confidentiality and Its Limits

Everything discussed in our sessions is strictly confidential. I will not share your information with anyone, including your GP, without your explicit consent.

However, under UK law and ethical safeguarding guidelines, there are rare exceptions where confidentiality must be broken. These include:

  • If I believe you are at serious, immediate risk of harm to yourself or others.
  • If you share information concerning the abuse or neglect of a child or vulnerable adult.
  • If I am legally compelled to do so by a court of law (e.g., acts of terrorism or money laundering).

If confidentiality needs to be breached, I will always make every effort to discuss this with you first.

4. Clinical Supervision

To ensure I am providing ethical and effective therapy, I attend regular clinical supervision with a qualified supervisor. While I may discuss our work, I use pseudonyms and omit identifiable details to ensure your identity remains completely anonymous. My supervisor is bound by the same strict codes of confidentiality.

5. Your Rights

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), you have the right to request access to the information I hold about you, request corrections if it is inaccurate, or request its deletion (though legal retention requirements may override this).

If you have any concerns about how your data is handled, please bring them to our sessions. You also have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.
